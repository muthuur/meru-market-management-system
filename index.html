Market History

The Meru Market has a rich history dating back to the early 1900s. It started as a small gathering of local farmers and has grown into a bustling marketplace that serves the entire region.
Through thesupport of the previous governments, the market has grown and marked its spot in the Kenyan economic map due to sales of food products like maize, beans, bananas, sweet potatoes, potatoes, arrow roots, french peas, tomatoes, onions and many more.
The growth of the market has been evident due to the sale of the green gold 'MIRAA' that brings 78% of the market income due to it's evergrowing demand and consuption countrywide. Miraa sales have the surpassed all other cash crops and is now creating 38% of job oppotunities in the Meru region.
Her Exelency Bishop Kawira Mwangaza has spoken about plans to improve the working conditions in the Meru market and has promised to build more affordable selling spots equiped with running water and token-enabled electricity that will favor the sellers. This comes after Her visit to the market and interacting will the traders.
The county Government of Meru has shown the importance of proper sanitation and has since built seven mordern toilets that are affordable to the sellers and costs a seller Ksh.10 to access. Water tanks (10) have been purchased to provide water incase the taps run dry.
